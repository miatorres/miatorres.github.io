I’m a designer at The Washington Post on the Emerging News Products team. This is the corner of the internet where I keep my work.


Those illustrations up there accompany stories curated for our national app, Washington Post Select. What ya gotta know about these is that I make a ton every day. Like at least 10. On deadline. It’s taught me how to conceptualize quickly and trust myself. (Click to see more)


These illustrations are a little different. They’re for The Lily, the Post’s women-focused publication. In between work on the app, I illustrate for Lily stories and help manage its Instagram.


I also art direct regularly for The Lily’s newsletter, Lily Lines. I get to collaborate with wildly talented artists on a wide range of topics —— from post-pandemic workplace conditions for mothers to the 50th anniversary of landing on the moon.


About a lifetime ago, I was at the Indiana Daily Student, Indiana University’s independent student-run newspaper. I worked as creative director of both the paper and magazine. There’s a lot I could show you from my time there —— like this interactive board game —— but I’ll just leave you with this gif I animated for a story about ghosting.


And wouldn’t you know, I’ve also worked in print. Before joining the Emerging News Products team and honing in on digital, print was my primary focus. I designed for nearly every section of the Post’s physical paper. And before that, I spent my weeknights shipping the Indiana Daily Student off to the printer. You can see some of my published work here and some unpublished work here.


Originally from Chicago, I relocated to DC in 2018 when I joined the Post. I’m a New York Times Student Journalism Institute alumna and a National Association of Hispanic Journalists member. If ya wanna chat, feel free to shoot me an email or hit me up on Twitter.
