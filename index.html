About a lifetime ago, I was at the Indiana Daily Student, Indiana University’s independent student-run newspaper. I had a bunch of roles — creative director, multimedia editor, magazine design director, section editor, design editor. There’s a lot I could show you from my time there (like this interactive board game), but I’ll just leave you with this gif I animated for a story about ghosting.


And wouldn’t you know, I’ve also worked in print. Before joining the Emerging News Products team and honing in on digital, print was my primary focus. I designed for nearly every section of the Post’s physical paper. And before that, I spent my weeknights shipping the Indiana Daily Student off to the printer. Originally from the Southeast Side of Chicago, I'm the oldest of four and also the shortest. I spent my elementary school years in an Illinois suburb just south of the city. I can trace my earliest design memory back to third grade. For Valentine's Day, instead of passing out store-bought cards, I wanted to make my own with Microsoft Paint. So for each of my twenty-something classmates, I carefully scrolled through all the clip art hearts and chose the one I felt matched their personality best.

We moved to Northwest Indiana right before I started high school. After the first day of Journalism 1 freshman year, I was all in. The rest is kinda history. I went on to study journalism at Indiana University and eventually relocated to DC in 2018 when I joined the Post. I’m a New York Times Student Journalism Institute alumna and a National Association of Hispanic Journalists member. If ya wanna chat, feel free to shoot me an email or hit me up on Twitter.
